Design, does this word brings any images to you? we live in the world where we have lots of professionals under this tag, and myself fortunate enough to be one of them.

John Maeda, the legendary designer who addresses himself as a product manager who learns data science says that “Design is always about selling things”

“Design is always about selling things” — John Maeda.

However, whenever I address myself as a user experience designer, most of the people relate my field of work with graphics designing or user interface designing.

What is design?

I just ran a search on the same query and the google surfaced me this :- “Design is a work process which has a user perspective and drives development based on your specific customers’ needs.” — svid.se

To make things short, the design is all about making things easy for customers and for stakeholders.

Let’s take an example on a retail store and see how design can help in a retail store, so that we can see how design not just about apps and websites.

Have you ever noticed in a retail store that you can take a packet of cookies for 2$ and beside that there is a bundle of 3 packets of cookies with 5$? and how many times you picked the 5$ bundle even though you just needed a single pack?

This is mentioned as “Decoy effect” by Dan Ariely. The decoy effect is the phenomenon whereby consumers will tend to have a specific change in preference between two options when also presented with a third option that is asymmetrically dominated.

In his book, Predicbly irrational, Dan also talks about how the economist.com used the same effect to get more subscribers towards an expensive pack.

Did you notice that in the left side with the presence print subscription(decoy,) the number of subscribers for Print & web subscription is 84%. While on the right side, in the absence of Print subscription, the number of subscribers for Print & Web Subscription is just 32%.

Did you ever thought that a simple piece of duct tape can double the amount of healthy food shoppers buy at the grocery store?

Collin Payne of New Mexico State University and pasted some yellow duct tape across the middle of a shopping cart, with a sign telling shoppers to put fruits and vegetables in one half and the other groceries in the other.

“We showed a 102 per cent increase in people buying fruits and vegetables, without showing a decrease in supermarket profitability,” Payne said. “Allowing retailers such as supermarkets to maintain their profits is important in achieving buy-in for these kinds of tools.” — Collin Payne.

This is important when we consider the value it would make to a customer and to the fact that the design helped to manipulate the customer behaviour.

If you noticed, In these examples the researchers manipulated the human psychology and the behavioural economics to get maximum profits for the stakeholders, in these case the newspaper company and the retail store.

Similar to this, the design is an collective effort which uses different disciples to solve an existing problem.
