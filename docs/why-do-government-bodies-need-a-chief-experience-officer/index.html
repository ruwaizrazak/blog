Why do government bodies need a Chief Experience Officer?

  • Ruwaiz Razak

    Read more posts by this author.

Ruwaiz Razak

6 Jan 2018 • 2 min read

Weall are aware about the fact that government bodies are the best sources to deliver the best for the public. I’m from India and we are so fortunate to have a democratic government which is defined by Abraham Lincoln as “Of the people, By the People, For the people”.

All the government bodies are purposed to solve a particular problem for the public. However, even though there are lots of Boards, Missions, Agencies to address various problems in the region, these are not really effective in solving the problem.

Govt bodies are not really effective in solving the problem.

People do asks me, even though there are lots of bodies like this why the problems aren’t solved? Well, we can answer this in many ways, we can blame these on the government, we can blame these on the Organisations, We can blame these on the people, and we can blame these on us too, and why? Because we are part of this society.

However, these issues can be well addressed and solved only when we understand the current position and the position we have to achieve. This is the part where an Experience Officer comes to play.

What is an Experience Officer? A Wizard?

At this point, you might feel that I am portraying an experience officer as Albus Dumbledore who can create or fix anything by a flick of the wand, But I’m not.

The Experience Officer should understand the problem and the ones associated to it.

The experience officer should be the one who is responsible for understanding the problem, then to identify which set of society this problem is affecting, then to understand why this problem is there in society and how people are reacting to it, and then creating a user map on how to solve the problem effectively.

The processes which I mentioned above could take a lot of time. However, the result would be worthy of it.

The UX tool kits that can be handy.

If you are not someone who understands UX, this is the moment in which you might think that I’m paranoid. But hold on, Most of the government agencies works towards a well defined mission which means they know their goals, but mostly they get lost in the execution.

This is the place where an experience officer have the role to play. The experience officer is meant to identify the problem and the tag the associated problems as well. There is an old saying that “A Problem left understood cant be solved.”

Hence the experience officer is meant to get details about the problems from the set of public who are identified as the target audience. Once he acquires the details regarding problem, then creating a user journey map would help in understanding most of the issues that would come up during the execution of the mission.

Also, the experience officer can have a look at the models that has been created to solve the identical problems in other places.

To summarise, a CXO can use
1. Stakeholder interviews
2. Establishing Key Audience
3. Building Personas
4. Creating Scenarios
5. Creating User Stories
6. Competitive Research
7. And Testing.

To solve a particular problem effectively.
